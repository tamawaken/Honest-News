How It Works

From source scoring to verifiable publishing

The News Meta turns a noisy news cycle into a readable, inspectable article by making the source mix, framing differences, and verification trail visible at every step.

1. Gather coverage across the spectrum

We start by pulling reporting from left, center, and right outlets on the same story so the final article is grounded in a real spread of editorial viewpoints rather than a single narrative lane.

2. Score the source mix

Each story gets a visible source-mix bar. Readers can see at a glance whether a synthesis leans on a balanced spread or on a narrower set of sources.

3. Synthesize a readable article

We publish a coherent article that prioritises shared facts, preserves unresolved tension points, and avoids pretending that contested reporting is already settled.

4. Summarise framing differences

Every story includes left, center, and right framing summaries so readers can compare emphasis, omission, and tone without needing to open six browser tabs.

5. Expose the verification trail

Each article points readers to a processing file that explains the source scoring, weighting approach, and verification materials used for integrity checks.

Example workflow

Story collected from 6 outlets
Source mix scored: 33% left / 34% center / 33% right
Balanced article synthesized from overlapping reported facts
Framing boxes generated for left / center / right emphasis
Processing trail published for verification
