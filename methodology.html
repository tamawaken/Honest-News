How We Analyze News

Source Selection

We aggregate articles from outlets across the political spectrum, geographic regions, and cultural backgrounds. Our goal is diversity, not neutrality — we show what different perspectives are saying, not what we think is "true."

Limitation: Source diversity is variable per topic. Some stories have more coverage from certain perspectives. We clearly label when sources are limited.

Bias Measurement

Bias is measured with repeatable scoring rules based on wording, framing, and source comparison.

Bias indicators are descriptive, not prescriptive. They help readers inspect framing differences rather than dictate conclusions.

Emotional Tone Detection

We detect emotional manipulation through linguistic analysis: loaded words, fear triggers, polarization patterns. This is measured, not labeled — you see the scores, not our interpretation.

Counter-Narrative Detection

By comparing multiple sources on the same topic, we automatically identify alternative framings and missing perspectives. If a story consistently frames something one way, we flag that.

Composite Balance Metric

Each article includes a composite balance metric derived from multiple measurable factors. It is a summary score, not a truth score.

The score helps compare coverage patterns across sources and topics.

Data Limitations

  • Analysis reflects source data quality and bias
  • Counter-narratives require multiple sources — limited for niche topics
  • Emotional detection is technical, not absolute truth
  • Bias vectors are descriptive, not prescriptive

Transparency Promise

Where signatures are available, you can verify in your browser that a result file has not been changed after publication.
